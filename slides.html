The Problem

Bankruptcy
Filing &
Write-Off

Compass Coffee filed for bankruptcy. SFS wrote off the full $512K as uncollectable.

Situation at Time of Write-Off
Compass Coffee entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings
Only one location had a UCC lien — providing a secured claim on that loan only
Remaining balances treated as general unsecured claims — typically pennies on the dollar in bankruptcy
SFS recorded a full write-off of $512,000 assuming the balance was unrecoverable
Standard bankruptcy recovery for unsecured creditors is often 0–10 cents on the dollar. The write-off was a reasonable, conservative accounting decision.

Result
$512K

Full recovery of written-off balance

Outside counsel's cross-collateralization argument held. SFS collected the complete outstanding balance from the bankruptcy sale proceeds — turning a $512K write-off into a full recovery.

Legal Basis
The single UCC lien, argued as cross-collateral, extended security interest to all five loans across Compass Coffee's locations.
Key Enabler
The bankruptcy sale deadline created a must-close condition — giving SFS meaningful leverage to enforce the full balance at the point of sale.
